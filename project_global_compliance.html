01 The Challenge

As the organization scaled, "Product Integrity" began to mean different things to different sites. The US facility prioritized speed, the EU facility prioritized documentation, and the APAC facility prioritized yield.

This misalignment created friction during tech transfer and supply chain handoffs. Material released at one site would be held up at another for "documentation gaps," creating a Quality Bottleneck that threatened global supply continuity.

02 The Governance Framework

I spearheaded the initiative to harmonize our Quality Management System (QMS) into a single, global standard.

Harmonization Strategy

We didn't just impose new rules; we built consensus. I formed a Global Governance Committee comprising Quality, Engineering, and Operations leads from all sites. We reviewed 50+ SOPs and aligned them to the strictest common denominator (usually EU GMP or FDA 21 CFR).

Digital Integration

We integrated these new standards into our ERP (SAP) and Quality (TrackWise) systems. By enforcing standardized data entry fields globally, we ensured that a batch record generated in Singapore was instantly readable and compliant in California.

Site Compliance Scores (Pre vs. Post Harmonization)

Audit Readiness

To stress-test the system, I designed a "Mock Audit" program where site leads would audit each other's facilities. This cross-pollination of knowledge upskilled the entire engineering workforce.

03 The Results

The impact went beyond just compliance; it improved operational velocity.

  • Unified Standard: Achieved a single global Quality Manual accepted by all sites.
  • Speed: Reduced average lot release time by 20% due to standardized documentation packages.
  • Regulatory Success: The subsequent FDA inspection resulted in Zero Critical Observations across the network.
